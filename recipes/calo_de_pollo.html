Callo De Pollo

Caldo de Pollo is a heartwarming Mexican chicken soup that's both nourishing and comforting. This traditional soup features tender chicken pieces, fresh vegetables, and aromatic herbs simmered in a flavorful broth. It's often served with lime wedges, fresh cilantro, and warm tortillas on the side. This soul-warming dish is particularly popular during cold weather or when fighting off a cold, but it's enjoyed year-round in Mexican households. Each region might have its variations, but the essence remains the same: a clear, rich broth with fall-off-the-bone chicken and chunky vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 1 whole chicken (about 3-4 lbs), cut into pieces
  • 2 large carrots, cut into large chunks
  • 3 potatoes, quartered
  • 2 ears of corn, cut into thirds
  • 2 zucchini, cut into large chunks
  • 1 large white onion, quartered
  • 4 garlic cloves, whole
  • 2 celery stalks, cut into large pieces
  • Fresh cilantro (1 bunch)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Water (enough to cover ingredients)

Steps:

  1. Clean and wash the chicken pieces thoroughly, removing excess fat
  2. Fill a large pot with water and add the chicken pieces, whole garlic cloves, bay leaves, and quartered onion
  3. Bring to a boil over high heat, then reduce to medium-low. Skim off any foam that forms on top
  4. Season with salt and pepper, then simmer for about 30 minutes or until chicken starts to become tender
  5. Add the carrots, celery, and potatoes to the pot. Continue cooking for 15 minutes
  6. Add the corn and zucchini. Cook for another 10-15 minutes until all vegetables are tender but not overcooked
  7. Tie the cilantro bunch with kitchen twine and add it to the pot for the last 5 minutes of cooking
  8. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed
