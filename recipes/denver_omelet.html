The Denver Omelet

The Denver Omelet is a hearty American breakfast classic that originated with railroad workers and chuck wagon cooks in the American West. This fluffy, folded omelet is stuffed with a savory mixture of diced ham, bell peppers, onions, and melted cheese. The combination creates a perfect balance of protein and vegetables, with the sweetness of sautéed onions and peppers complementing the salty ham. It's a diner favorite that's equally delicious for breakfast, brunch, or breakfast-for-dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 3 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons milk or cream
  • 1/4 cup diced ham
  • 1/4 cup green bell pepper, diced
  • 1/4 cup onion, diced
  • 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 2 tablespoons butter, divided
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Pinch of dried herbs (optional)

Steps:

  1. Melt 1 tablespoon butter in a small skillet over medium heat
  2. Add diced onions and peppers, sauté until softened (about 3-4 minutes)
  3. Add ham, cook for another 2 minutes until slightly crispy
  4. Remove filling mixture and set aside
  5. Wipe out the skillet for reuse
  6. In a bowl, whisk eggs with milk, salt, and pepper until well combined
  7. Heat the clean skillet over medium heat
  8. Add remaining tablespoon of butter and let it melt
  9. Once butter is bubbling but not brown, pour in the egg mixture
  10. As eggs begin to set, use a spatula to gently push cooked edges toward center
  11. Tilt pan to allow uncooked egg to flow to the edges
  12. When eggs are mostly set but still slightly wet on top, add the ham, pepper, and onion mixture to one half of the omelet
  13. Sprinkle cheese over the filling
  14. Using a spatula, carefully fold the empty half over the filled half
  15. Cook for another minute until cheese melts
  16. Slide onto a plate and serve
